Simple Syrup
Yields ~16 oz · 3–4 weeks refrigerated
baseessential
Par status:
Ingredients
Granulated white sugar1 cup
Water1 cup
Method
1
Combine sugar and water in a small saucepan over medium heat.
2
Stir constantly until the sugar is fully dissolved — about 2–3 minutes. Do not boil.
3
Remove from heat and allow to cool completely at room temperature.
4
Transfer to a clean glass bottle or jar and seal tightly.
5
Label with the date and store in the refrigerator.
💡 For a richer, thicker texture use a 2:1 ratio (2 cups sugar to 1 cup water). This is called "rich simple syrup" and is sweeter — use slightly less in recipes.

Cinnamon Simple Syrup
Yields ~14 oz · 2–3 weeks refrigerated
spicedfallwinter
Par status:
Ingredients
Granulated sugar1 cup
Water1 cup
Cinnamon sticks, broken in half3–4
Method
1
Combine sugar, water, and broken cinnamon sticks in a saucepan over medium heat.
2
Stir until sugar is fully dissolved, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
3
Simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the cinnamon to steep and infuse.
4
Remove from heat and let steep for an additional 20 minutes covered.
5
Strain through a fine mesh strainer, discarding cinnamon sticks.
6
Cool completely, bottle, label with date, and refrigerate.
💡 The longer you steep, the spicier it gets. For a subtler cinnamon note, strain at 5 minutes. Great in whiskey sours, old fashioneds, and hot cocktails.

Rosemary Simple Syrup
Yields ~14 oz · 2 weeks refrigerated
herbalsavoryspring
Par status:
Ingredients
Granulated sugar1 cup
Water1 cup
Fresh rosemary4–5 sprigs
Method
1
Combine sugar and water in a saucepan over medium heat, stirring until dissolved.
2
Add fresh rosemary sprigs once the sugar is dissolved.
3
Reduce heat and simmer gently for 5 minutes.
4
Remove from heat. Let the rosemary steep in the warm syrup for 15–20 minutes.
5
Taste — if the flavor is strong enough, strain immediately. For more intensity, steep longer.
6
Strain through a fine mesh strainer, cool, bottle, and refrigerate.
💡 Rosemary can turn bitter if overcooked. Keep the heat low and watch the steep time carefully. Pairs beautifully with gin, grapefruit, and lemon.

Lavender Simple Syrup
Yields ~14 oz · 2–3 weeks refrigerated
floralspringsummer
Par status:
Ingredients
Granulated sugar1 cup
Water1 cup
Dried culinary lavender buds2 tbsp
Method
1
Bring water to a simmer in a small saucepan. Add sugar and stir until dissolved.
2
Add dried lavender buds to the warm syrup.
3
Remove from heat immediately — do not boil the lavender.
4
Cover and let steep for 15–20 minutes, tasting at 15 minutes.
5
Strain through a fine mesh strainer lined with cheesecloth to catch all buds.
6
Cool, bottle, and refrigerate. The syrup should be a pale golden color.
💡 Use culinary-grade lavender only. Over-steeping makes it soapy. Start tasting at 10 minutes. Works beautifully with vodka, gin, lemon, and elderflower.

Honey Syrup (2:1)
Yields ~12 oz · 3–4 weeks refrigerated
baseessential
Par status:
Ingredients
Raw honey2 cups
Hot water1 cup
Method
1
Heat water until just below boiling (do not boil).
2
Pour hot water over honey in a bowl or measuring cup.
3
Stir vigorously until honey is fully dissolved and mixture is smooth.
4
Allow to cool completely before bottling.
5
Store in the refrigerator in a sealed glass container.
💡 A 2:1 ratio gives a thick, rich syrup. For a thinner version, use equal parts. Pairs beautifully with bourbon, rum, rye, and meads. Great in a Bee's Knees or Gold Rush.

Fresh Ginger Syrup
Yields ~14 oz · 2–3 weeks refrigerated
spicedtropical
Par status:
Ingredients
Granulated sugar1 cup
Water1 cup
Fresh ginger, peeled and sliced thin4 oz
Method
1
Slice fresh ginger thin — no need to be precise, just consistent thickness.
2
Combine sugar, water, and ginger slices in a saucepan over medium heat.
3
Stir until sugar dissolves, then bring to a gentle simmer.
4
Simmer for 15 minutes until syrup is slightly golden and smells spicy.
5
Remove from heat, cover, and steep for 30 minutes for extra kick.
6
Strain through a fine mesh strainer, pressing the ginger to extract all liquid.
7
Cool, bottle, and refrigerate.
💡 The more you press the ginger while straining, the spicier the result. For a milder syrup, strain without pressing. Excellent in mules, dark and stormies, and whiskey cocktails.

Hibiscus Syrup
Yields ~14 oz · 2–3 weeks refrigerated
floraltropicalsummertart
Par status:
Ingredients
Granulated sugar1 cup
Water1 cup
Dried hibiscus flowers½ cup
Method
1
Bring water to a boil in a small saucepan.
2
Add dried hibiscus flowers and reduce to a simmer for 5 minutes.
3
Add sugar and stir until completely dissolved.
4
Remove from heat, cover, and let steep for 20 minutes.
5
Strain through a fine mesh strainer, pressing the flowers gently.
6
The syrup will be a deep ruby red. Cool, bottle, and refrigerate.
💡 Hibiscus adds a tartness similar to cranberry. Adjust sugar slightly if you want it less tart. Spectacular in tequila cocktails, rum punches, and sparkling drinks.

House Grenadine
Yields ~16 oz · 3–4 weeks refrigerated
classicessentialtart
Par status:
Ingredients
100% pomegranate juice (POM or fresh)1 cup
Granulated sugar1 cup
Fresh lemon juice1 tsp
Orange flower water (optional)1 tsp
Method
1
Combine pomegranate juice and sugar in a saucepan over medium-low heat.
2
Stir constantly until sugar is fully dissolved. Do not boil — keep it below a simmer.
3
Once dissolved, remove from heat immediately.
4
Stir in fresh lemon juice and orange flower water if using.
5
Allow to cool completely, then bottle and refrigerate.
💡 Never boil grenadine — it loses its bright flavor and color. Real pomegranate grenadine tastes nothing like the bright-red commercial version. No maraschino cherries were harmed.

House Orgeat
Yields ~20 oz · 2–3 weeks refrigerated
tikinuttyclassic
Par status:
Ingredients
Raw blanched almonds2 cups
Granulated sugar1½ cups
Water1 cup
Orange flower water1 tsp
Almond extract (optional)½ tsp
Method
1
Toast almonds in a dry pan over medium heat until lightly golden and fragrant — about 5 minutes. Watch carefully.
2
Let almonds cool, then pulse in a food processor until coarsely ground (not flour).
3
Combine ground almonds and water in a saucepan, bring to a low simmer for 10 minutes.
4
Strain through cheesecloth, squeezing firmly to extract the almond milk.
5
Return liquid to the saucepan, add sugar, and stir over low heat until dissolved.
6
Remove from heat, stir in orange flower water and almond extract.
7
Cool, bottle, and refrigerate.
💡 The key is squeezing every drop of almond milk from the pulp — this is where all the flavor lives. Toasting the almonds first adds depth. Essential for Mai Tais, Army & Navy, and any tiki build.

House Falernum
Yields ~16 oz · 2–3 weeks refrigerated
tikispicedclassic
Par status:
Ingredients
Sugar1 cup
Water1 cup
LimesZest of 4
Cloves10 whole
Allspice berries½ tsp
Fresh ginger, grated2 tbsp
Almond extract¼ tsp
Method
1
Dry toast cloves and allspice in a pan for 1 minute until fragrant.
2
Combine water, sugar, lime zest, toasted spices, and ginger in a saucepan.
3
Bring to a low simmer, stirring until sugar dissolves.
4
Simmer 10 minutes, then remove from heat and steep covered for 1 hour.
5
Strain through a fine mesh strainer, pressing solids.
6
Stir in almond extract. Cool, bottle, and refrigerate.
💡 Falernum is the secret weapon of tiki cocktails. It bridges sweet, citrus, and spice in one ingredient. Adjust lime zest quantity to your preference for tartness.

Hot water1 cup
Method
1
Heat water until very hot but not boiling.
2
Combine hot water and demerara sugar in a container.
3
Stir vigorously until all sugar is dissolved — takes 3–4 minutes due to coarser grain.
4
Allow to cool completely. The syrup will be amber-colored and slightly thick.
5
Bottle and refrigerate.
💡 Demerara syrup has a rich molasses depth that regular simple syrup lacks. Essential for Old Fashioneds, Rum cocktails, and anything where you want caramel backbone.

Lavender Infused Vodka
Yields 750ml bottle · 6 months
floralspiritspring
Par status:
Ingredients
Neutral vodka (Titos, Ketel One, or similar)750ml
Dried culinary lavender buds3 tbsp
Method
1
Add dried lavender buds directly into the vodka bottle or a clean mason jar.
2
Seal tightly and store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight.
3
Shake gently once per day.
4
Taste starting at 24 hours — lavender infuses quickly.
5
Once the desired flavor is reached (usually 24–48 hours), strain through a fine mesh strainer lined with cheesecloth.
6
Return to a clean bottle, label with date, and store at room temperature or refrigerated.
💡 Less is more with lavender. Infusing too long or using too much will make it taste soapy. Start tasting at 12 hours if you prefer a subtle note.

Cucumber Infused Gin
Yields 750ml bottle · 1 week (flavor fades)
freshsummerspirit
Par status:
Ingredients
Gin (Hendricks or Botanist recommended)750ml
English cucumber, sliced thin1 medium
Method
1
Slice cucumber thin — about ⅛ inch rounds. No need to peel.
2
Add cucumber slices to a large mason jar and pour gin over them.
3
Seal and refrigerate (cucumber infusions should always be cold).
4
Taste at 4 hours — cucumber is delicate and infuses fast.
5
Strain at 6–8 hours maximum through a fine mesh strainer.
6
Return to a clean bottle and keep refrigerated. Use within 1 week.
💡 Unlike spirit infusions, cucumber infusions should be done cold and used quickly — fresh cucumber flavor fades fast. Do not infuse longer than 12 hours or it gets bitter.

Jalapeño Infused Tequila
Yields 750ml bottle · 3–6 months
spicyspiritsummer
Par status:
Ingredients
Blanco tequila750ml
Fresh jalapeños, sliced (seeds included for heat)2–3
Method
1
Slice jalapeños into rounds — keep seeds for more heat, remove for milder spice.
2
Add jalapeño slices to a clean jar and pour tequila over.
3
Seal and let sit at room temperature.
4
Taste every 30 minutes — heat level builds fast.
5
Strain when the heat level is right for your bar (typically 1–4 hours).
6
Strain through a fine mesh strainer and return to a clean bottle.
7
Label with heat level achieved and date.
💡 This one moves fast. Check every 30 minutes. The capsaicin from jalapeño is alcohol-soluble, meaning the longer it sits, the hotter it gets — and it keeps extracting even after straining.

Coffee Infused Bourbon
Yields 750ml bottle · 3–6 months
richspiritwinterfall
Par status:
Ingredients
Bourbon (mid-shelf recommended)750ml
Coarse-ground quality coffee beans, whole or coarse ground½ cup
Method
1
Add coffee beans or coarse ground coffee to a mason jar.
2
Pour bourbon over the coffee and seal tightly.
3
Let sit at room temperature for 12–24 hours, tasting every few hours.
4
Strain first through a coarse strainer, then through a coffee filter or cheesecloth.
5
The bourbon should be noticeably darker with a rich coffee aroma.
6
Return to a clean bottle and label with date.
💡 Use whole or coarsely ground beans — fine grind makes it bitter and hard to strain. A light to medium roast preserves the bourbon's sweetness. Phenomenal in espresso martinis and milk punches.
